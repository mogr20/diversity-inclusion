What is Diversity and Inclusion?

Diversity and Inclusion refers to, the range of people within a group, and that everyone within the group feels valued and accepted. The practices and tips on this website can help you meet the legal requirements within the Equality Act and discrimination law.

The following are the protected characteristics against discrimination:

  • Age
  • Disability
  • Gender Reassignment
  • Marriage and Civil Partnership
  • Pregnancy and Maternity
  • Race
  • Religion or Belief
  • Sex
  • Sexual Orientation

As well as an legal requirement, diversity and inclusion practices within an organisation can help lower the risk of; bullying, harrassment, and discrimination. When people are valued and an effort is made to include them, they are happier within your organisation and are more productive.

Tips

The following are some tips to help you create a more diverse and inclusive environment within your organisation:

Organisation policy

  • The organisation supports and treats everyone fairly.
  • What kind of behaviour is expected of them.
  • About discrimination and the law, and what is not acceptable.
  • Where to find the procedures for resolving any problems.

Communication

  • Talk openly with all organisation members.
  • Current state of the organisation meetings.
  • Clear communication about changes, decisions, or plans.
  • Open topic meetings to meet and ask senior staff questions.

Action plan

  • Staff training, and what is expected from them to ensure policies work.
  • Monitoring and measuring if the policy (and the action plan) is working as intended, and making records of this.
  • Clear policy goals, and procedures to change the policies if they don't meet expectations.
  • Who puts the policy into practice.

Recruitment

  • Advertise in at least two different places.
  • Split recruitment requirements into; essentials, and desirables.
  • Welcome all applications from those who believe they meet the essential requirements.
  • Consider and implement "positive action" for anyone under-represented in the organisation.
