Diversity

What is Diversity?

What is "Positive Action"?

Positive action under the UK Equality Act 2010 refers to measures that can be taken to help people from disadvantaged or underrepresented groups compete on more equal terms, without alienating or disadvantaging other groups. It allows employers and service providers to take steps to support these groups, such as providing targeted training or outreach programs, without falling foul of discrimination laws.

Positive action is intended to level the playing field and address disadvantages that certain groups may face, promoting greater equality and diversity within the workplace and society.

Why should organisations implement a positive action policy?

Implementing a positive action policy can help organisations create a more diverse and inclusive workplace. It can address imbalances and barriers that underrepresented groups face, leading to a more equitable environment. Additionally, diverse teams can bring varied perspectives and ideas, fostering innovation and improving decision-making. A positive action policy can also enhance the organisation's reputation, making it more attractive to potential employees and customers who value diversity and inclusion.

Implementing Positive Action

Organisations can implement positive action by taking the following steps:

  1. Identify Underrepresented Groups: Conduct an analysis to identify which groups are underrepresented or disadvantaged within the organisation.
  2. Set Clear Objectives: Define clear, measurable objectives for what the organisation aims to achieve through positive action.
  3. Develop Targeted Programs: Create programs such as mentorship, training, and outreach initiatives specifically designed to support underrepresented groups.
  4. Review Recruitment Practices: Ensure that job advertisements, selection criteria, and interview processes are inclusive and do not inadvertently disadvantage any group.
  5. Provide Training: Offer training for all employees on diversity, inclusion, and the importance of positive action to foster a supportive environment.
  6. Monitor and Evaluate: Regularly monitor the effectiveness of positive action measures and make adjustments as necessary to ensure they are meeting the set objectives.
  7. Communicate Commitment: Clearly communicate the organisation's commitment to positive action both internally and externally to build trust and transparency.

By following these steps, organisations can create a more inclusive and equitable workplace that benefits all employees.
